Unawatuna

Unawatuna is a major tourist attraction in Sri Lanka and known for its beach and corals. It is a suburb of Galle, about 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) southeast to the city center and approximately 108 kilometres (67 mi) south of Colombo. Unawatuna is situated at an elevation of 5 metres (16 ft) above the sea level.

Unawatuna is one of the biggest tourist destinations in Sri Lanka and is the most “famous” beach in the country. It is a lovely banana-shaped beach of golden sand and turquoise water, surrounded by green palm trees.At the west end of the beach, there are some rocks and stairs that lead up to a small hill with a pagoda and a big Buddha statue. It’s a nice little walk with a beautiful view overlooking the beach.

Andahalena Ella Waterfall

Andahelena Ella Waterfall is situated in Elipitiya town of Galle. It is 12 meters high and is located inside the jungle named Beraliya Mukalana (Beraliya Jungle). Vehicles cannot be taken close to the waterfall, so the final part of the journey should be completed on foot. The beauty of Andahelena Ella is best viewed and appreciated from close range which makes the difficult approach well worth the trouble.A picturesque pool is formed at the top of the waterfall before the water cascades downwards.

A canopy is formed in the surrounding area of the waterfall which consists of Kaluwara, Bakmee, Telkekuna, Wanasapu, Venivel, Ehela, Teak, Ankenda and Bata Domba trees.From Galle take the Southern Expressway and exit from Kurundugahahetekma and take the Galle – Elpitiya Road. From Elpitiya, take the Pituwala – Kahaduwa Road and travel about 5 km down this road to reach Andahelena Ella Waterfall.

Kanneliya Rain Forest

Kanneliya Forest Reserve Experience the feeling of walking in a tropical rain forest .The KDN Forest Reserve (Kanneliya-Dediyagama-Nakiyadeniya) is the second largest primary forest area in Sri Lanka and is renowned for its watershed value. The forest occupies the area between two rivers - the Gin Ganga and the Nilwala Ganga.The natural vegetation of the KDN complex is lowland wet evergreen forest, which is a climax forest formation dominated by an association of Shorea, Dipterocarpus and Mesua. Over 17% of the wet zone flora is confined to this area where the KDN forest is the most prominent. This region has been identified as one of the floristically richest areas in South Asia. The endemic flora and fauna of the southwestern wet zone, including the KDN, forms a relic of the biota of the Indian Plate with Gondwanaic ancestry. Therefore, the biota of southwest Sri Lanka, including the KDN forest complex, is of considerable scientific interest with respect to biogeography. There is a high proportion of endemism amongst the identified woody plants, with 150 species being endemic to Sri Lanka.There are 78 villages surrounding the KDN forest complex. There are small-scale cottage industries based on Fish-tail palm (Kitul), and many local people are involved in the collection of medicinal plants and plant products, as well as rattan, to produce handicrafts and furniture. What can be seen:- Over 100 bird species, 16 of which are endemic to Sri Lanka (such as the Green-billed Coucal, Blue Magpie and Spot-winged Thrush). Over 15 species of endemic fresh-water fish. Many other endemic reptiles, amphibians and butterflies can also be seen in the area

Thalpe Beach

Thalpe Beach, located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka near the city of Galle, is a captivating and enchanting destination that leaves a lasting impression on our hearts. It is a popular spot for both locals and foreigners, offering vibrant shops, restaurants, and a range of water sports activities in the surrounding area.Nestled away from the bustling towns along the southern coastal belt, Thalpe is a peaceful town that exudes history, adventure, culture, and cuisine. The beach itself is clean and serene, providing a tranquil escape from the chaos of everyday life. Stilt fishermen can often be seen along the coastline, offering a glimpse into the unique way of life in this charming coastal town.The rock pools, which were carved out of corals around 50 years ago, are a notable feature of Thalpe Beach. They were originally used for soaking coconut husks to extract coconut coir. The best time to visit these rock pools is early morning from March to April, as they tend to be filled with sand at other times. To reach Thalpe Beach, one can travel approximately 11.2km along the Matara Road from Galle.Nowadays, Thalpe Beach has become a highly sought-after destination for travelers, especially for those who have already explored the Galle fort. It offers a unique experience that is unparalleled in other parts of Sri Lanka. Many tourists are drawn to the rock pools, which provide a sense of wonder and amazement. A visit to this beach is sure to evoke a sense of awe and leave you with unforgettable memories. Thalpe Beach offers a distinct and delightful experience.The water color and sand at Thalpe Beach are particularly captivating, creating a visually stunning environment unlike any other beach area. The overall ambiance of Thalpe is truly impressive, evoking feelings of freedom, amazement, happiness, and pure joy. It is undoubtedly one of the most attractive places you can visit in Sri Lanka.

Doowili Ella Waterfall

Doowili Ella is the main tourism spot in the Neluwa tourism zone. This tourism spot is developed and maintained by the Neluwa Local Government council and southern Provincial Council. It has received great attention of both local and international tourists. This is not a single waterfall but a network of three waterfalls. This is about 100 feet in height and gets its name because of the water vapour that sprays throughout the waterfall that resembles dust (Doowili). The beauty of this waterfall can be witnessed during most part of the year because it is formed from a stream of water that begins at the Sinharaja rainforest reservation which is also a world heritage site. In order to reach the highest point of the waterfall a stairway with a safety fence has been built and as such a person of any age reach that point safely. Because this waterfall has about 6 acres of forest reservation, many of the plants and animal species inherent to Sinharaja rainforest can be seen here as well. Below the waterfall there are 2 ponds that have been built without any disturbance to its natural beauty for both adult and children bathe in.

Kottawa Reserve Forest

The Kottawa Forest Reserve is situated along the main road leading to Udugama Township in the northeast of Galle. Covering around 37 acres, this small section of low country rainforest exhibits all the typical characteristics of a rainforest. The lush vegetation is composed of wet evergreen plants, with towering trees and dense canopies that block sunlight from reaching the forest floor. This tropical forest, located between 50 and 300 meters above sea level, can be observed on both sides of the road. Upon entering the Kottawa Rainforest, visitors will encounter various pathways leading in different directions. One of these paths guides visitors to a tea plantation in Down country, where they can explore tea research facilities and observe freshly picked tea leaves. Many tourists opt to explore all these attractions in a single trip. Another pathway leads to a natural water tank, a popular spot for tourists to enjoy bathing. Moreover, the Kottawa Rainforest is renowned for its diverse bird population, attracting birdwatchers from far and wide. This forest is recognized as a climax community, indicating a positive outlook for its ongoing preservation. The trees are supported by buttress roots, and the understory is rich with dominant tree species. Tree ferns and Dipterocarpus species are abundant in the vegetation, while moss and orchids flourish on the towering tree trunks of these species.

Hikkaduwa Beach

Hikkaduwa,boasts one of the most beautiful beaches in the country. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of activities, from surfing to indulging in delicious seafood curries. In addition to water sports, you can also treat yourself to relaxing massages and explore the rich cultural heritage by visiting Buddhist temples in the area. Narigama Beach, stretching over 4 kilometres, is a popular spot in Hikkaduwa. It is known for its excellent surfing conditions from April to October, while the calm waters during the winter months make it ideal for snorkelling and swimming. Keep an eye out for giant turtles swimming along the coast, as this area is a prime location for turtle nesting. The pristine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters of Hikkaduwa Beach are truly mesmerizing. The Hikkaduwa Coral Sanctuary is dedicated to preserving coral formations that were devastated by the 2004 tsunami. By joining a snorkelling excursion, you can explore the vibrant coral reef and get up close to marine life, including turtles. Most tours include snorkels and masks, but it's advisable to confirm this before making a booking. Tour operators in Hikkaduwa are readily available to help you arrange a memorable snorkelling adventure.

Anagimale Ella Waterfall

The Anagimale Ella Waterfall can be found within the Kanneliya Forest Reserve in the district of Galle. To reach the waterfall, one must embark on a 3 km trek, which is fairly easy to navigate. The waterfall stands at an impressive height of 46 meters. Surrounding the Anagimale Ella Waterfall is an array of diverse flora and fauna, making it a vibrant sight, especially during the months of May to June.

The area around the waterfall is home to various species of orchids and butterflies, further enhancing its natural beauty. Notably, the Anagimale Ella Waterfall is conveniently situated near the entrance of the Kanneliya Forest Reserve, while another waterfall, known as the Narangas Ella Waterfall, is located deeper within the forest.

Madu ganga

The Madu Ganga is a small waterway that originates near Uragasmanhandiya in the Galle District of Sri Lanka. As it widens, it forms the Madu Ganga Lake at Balapitiya. After flowing for an additional 4.4 km (2.7 mi), it eventually drains into the Indian Ocean. This location is situated 88 km (55 mi) south of Colombo and 35 km (22 mi) north of Galle. The Madu River is renowned for its abundant biodiversity, particularly as it passes through the wet zone of Sri Lanka and opens up into the expansive Madu Lagoon before reaching the Indian Ocean.

One of the must-do activities for any Sri Lankan holiday-goer is the Madu River Safari, which offers a chance to explore the river's unique attractions. From the captivating Cinnamon Island to the lush mangroves that line the river, housing a variety of endemic plants and animals, this boat safari is a popular choice for visitors to the southern coast of Sri Lanka, particularly in Bentota.

The Madu Ganga Lake, along with the smaller Randombe Lake, forms the Madu Ganga wetland, connected by two narrow channels. This wetland is home to a complex coastal ecosystem, with its estuary and numerous mangrove islets. It holds great ecological, biological, and aesthetic significance, boasting approximately 303 plant species from 95 families and 248 vertebrate animal species. The islet inhabitants are involved in the production of peeled cinnamon and cinnamon oil. Recognizing its importance, the Madu Ganga Wetland was officially declared a Ramsar site in 2003.
