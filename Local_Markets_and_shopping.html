Ambalangoda Mask Museum

The tradition of Sri Lankan Masks has been in existence since ancient times. Mask carving is a local custom in the southern coastal region of Sri Lanka, particularly in Ambalangoda, known for its traditional masks carvings and masks dancing. The renowned institution 'Ariyapala Traditional Masks' has a history dating back many centuries and has reached perfection in the hands of the Wijesooriya family in Ambalangoda. The Ariyapala Tradition has been passed down through seven generations of traditional masks carving and Low country dancing within the family. The cultural legacy of this family has become an integral part of Ambalangoda's history. "Ariyapala Wijesooriya Gurunnanse" is considered one of the greatest master craftsmen in Sri Lanka, who passed on his extensive knowledge of traditional masks carving and dancing to Mahindapala Wijesooriya, an acclaimed master carver who has received national and international awards.

Cinnamon Island

The island has been under the ownership of the same family for over a century. They grow cinnamon without the use of any chemical or artificial fertilizers, sticking to traditional methods passed down through generations. Upon arrival, the joyful sounds of children playing can be heard in the distance. Walking towards a small house, the welcoming family greets you with warm smiles. After a brief tour of the island, the locals invite you to their home and seat you on the porch. A cup of cinnamon tea is served, promising to rejuvenate your body, mind, and soul. While enjoying the tea, the eldest family member shares the island's history and the art of cinnamon farming, demonstrating how cinnamon is extracted from tree bark. They offer a variety of oils and cinnamon for you to experience, providing a full sensory journey.

Galle Fort Market

Situated in the renowned Galle Fort, this marketplace showcases a variety of goods like indigenous handicrafts, jewelry, garments, spices, and mementos. Discover exclusive items like handcrafted textiles, batik materials, authentic masks, and detailed woodwork. It serves as an excellent venue to engage with local culture and connect with skilled craftsmen.
