Rumansala

Situated a little over 5 kilometers away from Taru Villas' properties in Galle Fort, the Rumassala Hill is a renowned attraction and a natural marvel that is surrounded by mythical tales. According to the chronicle of the Ramayana, the hill is believed to be a fragment of the Himalayan Mountain Range, where unique and exotic flora, typically found only in the Himalayas, thrives abundantly.

Recognized as a wildlife sanctuary, Rumassala Hill serves as a sanctuary for both native and migratory birds, making it an ideal destination for bird-watching enthusiasts. If you wish to explore this captivating location, feel free to contact our team for guidance on how to reach there!

Underwater Museum

The inaugural of Sri Lanka's first underwater museum took place on June 17, 2020, in the coastal area of Galle, the capital city of the Southern Province. Galle is renowned as one of Sri Lanka's premier tourist destinations. The Sri Lanka Naval Force, under the guidance of the Sri Lankan government, successfully executed this remarkable underwater museum project. Vice Admiral Piyal De Silva, the Commander of the Sri Lanka Navy, officially inaugurated the underwater museum. This addition to Sri Lanka's museum collection is a testament to their commitment to preserving their rich cultural heritage. The primary goal of the Sri Lanka Underwater Museum is to attract tourists to the country. Numerous international travel journals have recognized Sri Lanka as one of the world's top travel destinations. As an island nation, Sri Lanka has a unique opportunity to develop maritime-based tourism. Another significant objective of the underwater museum is to create an artificial coral reef over time, thereby enhancing the marine life in the Galle area. This initiative not only promotes ecological conservation but also provides a captivating experience for visitors to witness the beauty of the underwater world.

Madol Duwa

Koggala is one of the famous tourists’ attraction places in Sri Lanka. It is located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka. Madol Duwa is one of seven islands located on the Koggala Lake. It is a biodiversity place where you can enjoy boat trips in the lagoon and koggal lake.

Madol Duwa is an island whereas became very well-known through the famous ‘Madol Duwa’ noval written by Martin Wickramasinghe. You can to take boat trips and explore the islands around the Koggala Lake. It is a home to various species of mangrove swamps, prawns and birds..
